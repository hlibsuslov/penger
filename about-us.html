[ THE ORIGIN ]

Why PENGER Exists

Self-custody is the foundation of financial sovereignty. Yet the most critical element — the seed phrase — remains vulnerable. Paper degrades. Metal stamps misalign. Digital backups introduce the very attack surfaces they claim to eliminate.

PENGER was born from a simple realization: the crypto industry had solved private key generation, wallet encryption, and transaction signing — but left the physical backup as an afterthought. We set out to engineer a backup solution with the same rigor applied to the cryptography itself.

The result is an aerospace-grade titanium plate with a binary encoding system that eliminates human error, resists extreme environments, and requires zero digital tools to create or restore.

[ OUR MISSION ]

Making Self-Custody Permanent

We believe that true ownership means holding your own keys — and that holding your own keys means having a backup you can trust for generations. Our mission is to make seed phrase security accessible, permanent, and error-proof for every digital asset holder.

[ PRINCIPLES ]

What We Stand For

Zero Digital Footprint

No apps, no Bluetooth, no WiFi, no firmware. The encoding process is entirely offline and manual. Your seed phrase never exists in digital form during backup.

Engineered for Extremes

Grade 2 titanium withstands 1,668°C, resists corrosion, and survives conditions that destroy every other backup medium. Built to outlast the owner.

Error-Proof by Design

The BIP39 binary grid with built-in checksum verification eliminates transcription errors. Every word maps to an 11-bit sequence — unambiguous, verifiable, permanent.

Open Standard

Built on the BIP39 standard used by every major hardware and software wallet. No proprietary formats, no vendor lock-in. Your backup works with the entire ecosystem.

[ THE PRODUCT ]

PENGER v1.0

Each PENGER kit includes a precision-machined titanium plate, a center punch tool, and a complete encoding guide. The plate features a 24-word binary grid aligned with the BIP39 wordlist — 2,048 words mapped to 11-bit binary sequences.

The encoding process is simple: look up each word's index, convert to binary, and punch the corresponding dots. No electronics, no software, no third party ever sees your phrase. Restoration follows the same process in reverse.
